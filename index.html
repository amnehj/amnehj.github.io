About Me

I am a mechanical engineer with a master’s degree from Colorado School of Mines and two years of combined industrial and academic research experience. Over the past few years, I have worked on a varied set of projects ranging from the thermal modeling of an electric car battery cooling system, the fluid dynamic analysis of a Francis turbine, to designing and programming a balancing robot.

After receiving my bachelor’s degree, I worked at Airport International Group - QAIA as a Mechanical engineer Intern where I received hands on HVAC engineering training. I later relocated to Turkey and worked as a project engineer at Daggaz where I designed piping and instrumentation diagrams (P&ID) for odorizing and chemical injection systems. In 2018, I joined the graduate engineering program at Colorado School of Mines and received a master’s degree in mechanical engineering – thermal fluid systems. Shortly after starting grad school, I began working on a building energy modeling research project under the supervision of one of my professors, the work went on for a little over a year and expanded into an open studio validation study covering multiple school of mines building models, and utilizing building automation system data for parameter estimation.
